Remote pairing in mac (with low latency)

Prelude

The company I work for is a remote first company. Most of my colleagues are not in the same place and we get to work on different projects based on the need, skills and interests of the individuals. That means, we need to have an effective way to pair program. We don’t do pair programming as a rule but to deal with complex stories and exchange implementation ideas. We also find pairing to be an effective mechanism to onboard new developers into the project

So, how do we do that? What tools do we use? Of course there are tons of options out there to screen share (Slack, Google Meet, Skype, etc) but when it comes to remote controlling other developer’s screen, there are not many. We found a setup that works well with low latency and I would like to share it here.

TL;DR

Use Mac screen sharing with ngrok tcp tunnel opened in your region (for minimal network latency). Read on if you want to know how to do it.

Setup

Suppose you wanted to share your desktop with your pair, do the following in your mac

  1. Enable “Remote Management”

Enabling remote management in your mac allows other machines to connect to your machine via the default port 5900

Also click on “Computer Settings…” and enable “Anyone may request permission to control screen”. This makes it easy for your pair to simply request permission instead of you sharing your login username and password.

  1. Start ngrok

ngrok is a very interesting tool to expose your local ports securely to the outside world. If you have not used it before, head here to set it up in your machine and play around. It should not take more than 5 mins to see it in action

Once you have ngrok command line tool in your mac, Run the following command

ngrok tcp -region ap 5900

We are exposing the tcp port 5900 via ngrok. The -region option is used to pick the ngrok server in Asia Pacific (one closer to me) so that there is lesser network latency. Once you run the above command, you should see the output with a line similar to one below

Forwarding tcp://0.tcp.ap.ngrok.io:17825 -> localhost:5900

This is the hostname with which your pair can connect to your machine

  1. Ask your pair to connect

Share the hostname and port (0.tcp.ap.ngrok.io:17825 in the above example) to your pair and ask him to open the “Screen Sharing” app and enter it

Then you will be shown the below screen so that you can “Request permission”

Once your pair clicks on connect, you will see a popup requesting you to grant permission to this new connection. Once you allow it, your pair can start controlling the machine with his mouse and keyboard.

Happy Remote Pairing!
